Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies

Your guide to understanding blockchain and popular cryptocurrencies.

What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a decentralized ledger technology that allows secure and transparent transactions without the need for a central authority. It is the backbone of cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum.

Popular Cryptocurrencies

Bitcoin (BTC)

Bitcoin is the first decentralized cryptocurrency, introduced in 2009 by an unknown person or group of people under the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. It is often referred to as "digital gold" due to its limited supply of 21 million coins.

Ethereum (ETH)

Ethereum is a decentralized platform that enables smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps) to run without any downtime, fraud, or interference. It introduced the concept of programmable blockchain.

Ripple (XRP)

Ripple is a real-time gross settlement system, currency exchange, and remittance network created by Ripple Labs. Its cryptocurrency, XRP, aims to enable secure, instant, and nearly free global financial transactions.

Solana (SOL)

Solana is a high-performance blockchain supporting fast and low-cost transactions. It is designed to host decentralized applications and scalable crypto projects.
